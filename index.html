The World Wide Web

Developed by Tim Berners-Lee at CERN, the WWW transformed the internet (which existed before) from a network primarily used by academics and researchers into a global, accessible information space for everyone. By creating foundational technologies like HyperText Markup Language (HTML), Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP), and Uniform Resource Identifiers (URIs/URLs), the Web made it possible to link and browse information seamlessly across the globe.

Artificial Intelligence

While the concept of AI is old, the last few decades have seen a monumental shift from theoretical ideas to practical, highly impactful applications. Projects like IBM's Deep Blue defeating a world chess champion in 1997, Google DeepMind's AlphaGo mastering the game of Go in 2016, and the recent explosion of Large Language Models (LLMs) like OpenAI's GPT series, Google's Gemini, and other generative AI systems, represent a new era.
